# Steph Speaks — Approach Patterns Audio Guide

## Opening Narration

This is a study of Steph Speaks' cold approach style — how he creates intrigue, builds connection quickly, and gets numbers. The goal isn't to become Steph. It's to absorb his patterns, his confidence, his flow — and make the parts that resonate yours.

Three principles before we start. One: rejection isn't real. You make it real by not approaching enough. Two: it doesn't matter what you say — it matters how you say it. Women respond to how you make them feel, not your words. Three: the fear you feel before approaching is your most powerful growth tool. Embrace it.

Now — let's listen to how approaches actually sound.

## Approach One: The Grocery Store

Setting: Grocery store. She's shopping. He walks up.

Steph: "Excuse me. I'm sure you get this told to you all the time, but I had to come up and say I think you're really pretty."

She's surprised. Flattered. Says thank you, that's so sweet.

He follows immediately: "You got good style too."

She's warming up. He pivots to a question — "You look Russian. Are you Russian?" She says no. He says, "I'm Russian, born in Latvia."

Now she's curious about HIM. She's asking questions. He's not chasing — she's leaning in.

He checks: "I don't want to waste your time. Do you have a man?"

She does. His response — listen to this — "That's good. That's how girlfriends need to act. Very good. Pleasure talking to you, Sydney."

Clean exit. No bitterness. Compliments her loyalty. She's smiling as he walks away.

**What happened:** Direct opener. Specific compliment. Created intrigue with his background. Asked about boyfriend directly. Exited clean when she was taken.

## Approach Two: The Street — Macy

He's walking. Sees a woman. Walks up.

"What's your name?"

"Macy."

"Macy, I like your style." He points to what she's wearing. "Where'd you get the glasses? Let me guess... Amazon."

She laughs. "TikTok Shop."

He laughs too. Then — watch the move: "Let me grab your number. I got to go get food."

No hesitation. No "would you maybe..." He says "let me grab your number" like it's the most natural thing in the world. And he creates urgency — he's got somewhere to be.

She says okay. Asks if he lives here. He says Miami. She was just there. Now they have a connection point.

He says: "You'll visit me and then you can see your cousin when you're with me."

Future projection. He's already painting a picture of them together.

"Text me."

"I will. I'm more of a caller though."

That one line — "I'm more of a caller" — separates him from every guy who fires off a "hey" text and waits three days. He's telling her he's different.

"Nice to meet you, Macy." And he's gone.

**What happened:** Name early. Playful tease about the glasses. Number close stated as fact, not question. Time constraint. Future projection. "I'm a caller" — differentiation.

## Approach Three: The Group — Ingred

He sees a group of women. Walks up to all of them.

"Hey guys, quick question. Are all of you good friends?"

"Yeah."

"Okay. So, would you be really happy or really jealous if something really good happened to one of your friends?"

They're confused. "Depends..."

"I'm just asking because I think you're very sexy."

Directed at one woman. Ingred. Her friends react. She thanks him.

"What's your name?"

"Ingred."

"Put your number in my phone quickly before you go back."

He hands her the phone. Number screen already open. Then he says the line that makes this approach work:

"Ignore your friends. Don't look at them. Look at me."

She looks up at her friends nervously. He holds steady.

"They can wait. They can wait. Just don't look up. They can wait."

She types in her number.

"You could call me crazy, but I want you all to myself. That's it."

Hug. He leaves. Her friends are buzzing.

**What happened:** Addressed the group first. Created a setup question. Isolated his target with the compliment. Handed her the phone — didn't ask. "Ignore your friends, look at me" — creates a private world in a public moment. Bold statement of interest at the end.

## Approach Four: The Bar Patio — The 29-Year-Old

She's sitting outside a bar. Her friends are inside.

He walks up: "Are those your friends inside?"

"Yes."

"I took my chance to come up and talk to you. I find you attractive. What's your name?"

She says he looks young. He says, "I am young." They do the age-guessing game. She's 29.

Then — "I go after what I see."

She asks what his type is. "Attractiveness."

She pushes back, tries to give him Instagram. He says:

"I don't have an Instagram. I don't do that."

She says he's lying. He counters: "You're this pretty and you don't have an Instagram?"

She says she doesn't need one. He says: "Can we put this down? I like to be face to face."

She moves the camera, they sit facing each other. He asks what she thinks he does for a living. She guesses model. He says that doesn't flatter him.

"Would you be shocked if I told you I don't do this often?"

She says she knows that's a lie. But she's engaged. She's invested.

He shakes all her friends' hands before he leaves: "I'm just shaking all of your hands because I got your friend's number."

**What happened:** Chance-based framing ("I took my chance"). Direct statement of interest. Age play. "I go after what I see." No Instagram — phone numbers only. "Face to face" — positions himself as present. Greeted the whole group on exit.

## Approach Five: Miami — The Masterclass

Setting: Streets of Miami. He spots two women walking.

"Hey, excuse me. You look too sexy to not be spoken to, so I had to come up and say hi."

"Hi. What's your name?"

"My name is Stephanie." 

"You go to Miami?"

"I do. You?"

"Yeah. I start in the spring."

He asks about her major. They connect on school. Then:

"Let me grab your number in case I don't see you there."

She offers Snapchat. He says: "No, I'm a number kind of guy."

She gives the number. Then he does something smooth — turns to her friends: "I'm going to talk to your friends for a little bit. What are y'all's names?"

He's not just getting the number and running. He's meeting the whole crew. Making them allies. When the friends like you, the number actually gets answered.

**What happened:** Bold opener. Fast name exchange. School as connection point. Number-only policy. Befriending the group.

Planting a seed. Not forcing it. Leaving the door open.

**What happened:** Adapted to the situation. Didn't push when it wasn't appropriate. Planted a future seed. "You can't blame me" — confident without being aggressive.

## Approach Seven: The Encounter That Gets Deeper — Alejandra

This is a longer interaction. Miami, on the street.

He opens and they quickly discover a connection — she's from Mexico, he's from Latvia, both travelers.

"Maybe you could show me around and then I'll show you around."

She laughs. Sounds good. They shake on it.

Then he says: "Let's make it romantic. You know, we have to look at each other in the eyes when we do this, right? Because if we don't, it's a broken promise."

They lock eyes during the handshake. The energy shifts.

"You got me feeling some kind of way. I'm being honest with you. You got me starstruck."

He's not performing cool detachment. He's expressing genuine attraction while maintaining confidence. There's a difference between "I don't care" and "I'm drawn to you and I'm not afraid to say it."

He gets the number. Then as they're walking, he flags down a passing police officer:

"Officer, do we look like a good couple?"

Making it playful. Making it an adventure. Making her feel like they're already in something together.

**What happened:** Travel connection. Eye-contact handshake ritual. Vulnerable honesty — "you got me starstruck." External validation play with the officer. The whole thing felt like the beginning of a story, not a pickup.

## The Deeper Pattern

Strip away the youth energy and the camera, and what's actually happening in every one of these approaches is this:

He walks up. He tells the truth — that he finds her attractive. He asks her name. He finds a connection point — anything. Background, style, travel, school. He makes a small joke or tease. He gets the number — not by asking, but by assuming it's happening. He creates a picture of the future. And he leaves before it gets stale.

The whole thing, every time, takes 60 to 120 seconds.

He's not manipulating anyone. He's being direct about what he wants. He's giving her an easy way to say yes, and an easy way to say no. When she says no, he leaves clean. When she says yes, he leaves while the energy is high.

That's the whole thing.

## Adapting This for You

You're not 23. You're not approaching 8,000 women. You don't need to be.

Your version of this is:
- Walking up to a woman who caught your eye at an event, a coffee shop, the marina
- Saying something direct and real: "I noticed you and I'd regret not saying something"
- Finding the connection point (you have great ones — sailing, travel, the water)
- Getting her number, not her Instagram
- Saying "I'll call you" and meaning it
- Leaving while she's still smiling

You already have the calm confidence. You already have the interesting life. You already have the close-quarters skill — reading a room, reading a person, staying grounded under pressure.

The only piece you're adding is the willingness to walk up and say the first sentence out loud.

That's it. The rest is already you.
