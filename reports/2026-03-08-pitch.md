## The Idea
Build a "Zip Code First" signup flow for SailorSkills Marketplace — ask for the marina first, derive everything else.

## Why Now
The "Zip Code First" pattern just hit 336 points on HN (250 comments) — the principle is simple: ask for the most information-dense field first, auto-fill everything downstream. For SailorSkills, the marina *is* the zip code. When a boat owner types "Berkeley Marina," you instantly know their location, typical boat sizes, available services, local providers, and even dock/slip conventions. Same for providers: entering a marina unlocks their service area, likely certifications needed, and potential clients. This maps directly to the provider onboarding flow Marcel just built — adding marina-first autocomplete would turn a multi-field form into a single-input flow.

## What It Looks Like
- Marketplace signup starts with one field: "Which marina?"
- Autocomplete from existing marina database (530+ provider locations already loaded)
- Selecting a marina auto-fills: city, state, service area, available services
- For boat owners: shows providers already active at their marina
- For providers: shows boats in the area and service demand signals
- Works for the `/get-listed` flow Marcel built yesterday and the owner-facing signup
